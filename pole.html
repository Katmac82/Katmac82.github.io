Pole Fitness

Pole Classes are broken down into different styles and categories based on the type of fitness you are looking for. If traditional dance and ballet is what moves you, check out our Polé class. If you like to get sexy and slinky, Pole Dance is for you. And if conditioning and strength training is your goal, try taking our Pole Fitness classes. All students must take an Intro to Pole class before beginning any level one Pole class.

  • Level 1:

    Great for any fitness level! Level 1 will introduce steps, spins, and transitions, while getting familiar with the pole.

  • Level 2:

    Adding more advanced spins and transitions to those covered in level 1, and more intense strength training, preparing students for more difficult combos and tricks. Preparation for climbing and inverting.

  • Tricks and Inversions 3:

    Building on the basic Inversion you learned in Intro to Inversion, next comes the fun stuff. Tricks and combinations you will learn include leg hangs, beginner handsprings, hip, elbow, and knee holds, and more!

  • Tricks and Inversions 4:

    Now that you have mastered your level 3 tricks, we will kick it up a notch with drops, intermediate handsprings with grip variations, and dead lifts. Bring your water, this is going to be a work out!

Pole Circuit

Strength building exercises, cardio endurance and flexibility training to enhance stamina and strength.

Hips to Heels

A class for the a**! Students will use every muscle from the waist down! Incorporating strength, cardio and dance. Bring your heels!

Pole Silks

A great fusion of Aerials and Pole. Long fabrics are attached to the top of the pole creating a secondary apparatus excellent for spotting pole moves, building strength, and creating gorgeous shapes.

PeeWee Polers

Pole fitness for kids! Ages 5-13, kids will learn team work, exercise, flexibility, and listening skills. It is so important to learn how to be active and healthy!
